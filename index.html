About Me

I am a PhD student in Mathematics at the university of Abderrahmane Mira, Béjaïa, where my research focuses on Cellular Automata, Symbolic Dynamics, Ergodic Theory, and Discrete Dynamical Systems. Alongside my research, I teach mathematics at Institution Sainte-Marie d'Antony in France. I am passionate about both research and teaching, and I enjoy sharing mathematical concepts with students while pursuing new developments in the theory of cellular automata.

My current research interests include the correction of 3-colourings by cellular automata, the dynamical properties of cellular automata, and their applications to mathematical and real-world modelling. More generally, I am interested in symbolic dynamics, the topological dynamics and the ergodic theory of cellular automata. I welcome opportunities for research collaborations and the exploration of new research directions in these areas and related fields.
